# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Adults in Calgary and across Alberta looking for a therapist for themselves, their teen or child, their relationship, or their family. They often arrive anxious, overwhelmed, grieving, burnt out, or unsure whether therapy is "for them." Many are first-time therapy-seekers doing nervous research late at night or on a phone. Their context: high emotional load, low patience for jargon or sales pressure, scanning for two things — "do these people feel safe and human?" and "can I actually book and afford this?"

The job to be done: find a therapist they trust enough to book a free intro call with, quickly and without friction.

## Product Purpose

Tri Lotus Psychotherapy is an in-person (Calgary, Sierra Morena/Signal Hill area) and Alberta-wide online therapy clinic serving adults, teens, children, couples, and families. The site exists to help a hurting person feel met and understood, build enough trust to take the first step, and route them cleanly to a free 20-minute intro call or a JaneApp booking.

Success = a visitor feels "these people get it, and I could see myself here," then books an intro call or contacts the clinic. Every section should lower the activation energy of reaching out.

## Brand Personality

Warm, grounded, human, credible. Three words: **calm, real, capable.** The voice is a relatable expert, not a clinical authority and not a wellness influencer. It validates ("all pain is valid and worthy of care") without coddling, and pairs compassion with practical, evidence-based competence. Emotional goal: the felt sense of exhaling — safety, spaciousness, and quiet confidence that change is possible.

## Anti-references

- **Generic therapy-stock cliché** — blurry stock imagery, lotus/zen-stone overload, purple gradients, cursive script logos, "your journey to wellness" filler.
- **Cold & corporate** — sterile clinical/hospital sterility, navy-and-grey, institutional or impersonal layouts.
- **Loud & salesy** — urgency, hype, big-promise marketing energy, neon CTAs, pop-up overload.
- **Trendy & cluttered** — over-animation, glassmorphism everywhere, busy gradient-mesh backgrounds, AI-slop maximalism.

## Design Principles

1. **Lower the activation energy.** The hardest thing the user does is reach out. Make the free intro call the calm, obvious, low-stakes next step everywhere, never pushy.
2. **Real humans, not archetypes.** Lead with the actual therapists, real warmth, plain language. Show the people and the room, not metaphors.
3. **Calm is the product.** The interface itself should feel like the relief therapy offers: spacious, unhurried, soft-edged, never overstimulating. Restraint is on-brand.
4. **Validate, then equip.** Pair emotional validation with concrete, evidence-based competence (named modalities, clear specialties) so the visitor trusts both the heart and the skill.
5. **Frictionless wayfinding.** Anyone (anxious teen's parent, burnt-out adult, couple) should find "the therapist / service for me" and a way to book within a couple of clicks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. This is a mental-health audience that may include users in distress, on phones, with anxiety or attention differences, so: high text contrast, large comfortable tap targets, calm low-stimulation motion with full `prefers-reduced-motion` support, no autoplaying motion or sudden movement. LGBTQ+ affirming and visibly inclusive; Treaty 7 land acknowledgment retained. Semantic headings, keyboard-navigable nav and booking links, descriptive alt text on therapist photos.
